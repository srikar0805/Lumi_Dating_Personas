Why we built Lumi.

One profile isn't enough. We're building a platform that matches the different sides of you to the people who meet those sides.

The problem

Every existing social and dating platform reduces people to a single flat profile — a photo, a bio, and a handful of tags. This creates a fundamental mismatch: people are multidimensional, but the systems built to connect them are not.

A person who is adventurous on weekends and deeply focused during the workweek will struggle to represent both sides in one Tinder bio. Most people give up and pick one version of themselves to present — usually the one they think the algorithm will reward.

Our answer

Lumi separates identity into intentional layers. You create multiple named personas — a Creative Self, a Career Self, a Weekend Self. Each has its own traits, interests, and goals. Matches happen between personas, not between people.

The question we set out to answer isn't "Are these two people compatible?" It's "Which version of each person connects — and why?"

What we wanted to achieve

Connection in context

Match people based on which version of themselves is showing up — not a single static profile.

Honest self-representation

Let users name the sides of themselves instead of squeezing every part of who they are into one bio.

Beyond romance

Find creative collaborators, travel companions, study partners, and mentors — anywhere "which side of me" matters.

Transparent matching

An interactive graph plus AI-generated compatibility reports show why two personas click.

How it works

  1. Build up to 5 selves. Name each one. Add traits, interests, mood, and a connection goal.
  2. A matching engine scores every persona pair on trait overlap, interest similarity, and goal alignment.
  3. A compatibility graph visualizes the network. Thicker lines mean stronger fits.
  4. An AI model generates a short narrative explaining the dynamic between any two matched personas.
